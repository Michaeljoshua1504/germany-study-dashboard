📍 Your jurisdiction: As an applicant based in Bangalore, Karnataka, your student visa is processed by VFS Global Shivajinagar, Bangalore on behalf of the German Consulate General, Bengaluru (which covers Karnataka & Kerala). Book your visa appointment through the Consular Services Portal / VFS as soon as you have an admission letter and blocked account confirmation.
Phase 0 — Before You Apply (mostly done) ▶
Foundational documents every Indian applicant needs before a German university or the embassy will even look at your file.
Phase 1 — After Receiving an Admission Letter (Zulassungsbescheid) ▶
The clock starts here. This letter is the foundation document everything else depends on.
Phase 2 — Financial Proof: Blocked Account (Sperrkonto) ▶
2026 requirement: €11,904 deposited up front, released at €992/month after you arrive. Open this 6–8 weeks before your visa appointment.
Phase 3 — Health Insurance ▶
You need proof of insurance before the visa appointment, then a switch to statutory cover once you arrive.
Phase 4 — Student Visa Application (VFS Bangalore → Consulate Bengaluru) ▶
One-shot process — note that the remonstration (appeal) procedure for visa rejections was abolished from July 2025, so the file needs to be complete and correct the first time.
Phase 5 — Pre-Departure Preparation ▶
Once the visa is in hand, the focus shifts to logistics.
Phase 6 — First 2 Weeks in Germany (legally time-critical) ▶
Germany has hard legal deadlines here — don't let these slip.
Phase 7 — Residence Permit & Settling In ▶
Your entry visa needs to be converted into a residence permit to stay for the full program.
Phase 8 — Looking Ahead ▶
Beyond the degree — this is your long-term DevOps career runway.
⚠️ Always double-check live requirements. Blocked account amounts, visa fees, and consulate procedures are reviewed periodically by German authorities — confirm current figures on auswaertiges-amt.de ↗ and aps-india.de ↗ before paying anything.
